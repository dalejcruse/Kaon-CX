Meet Alex.
He developed a need.
Luckily for Alex this isn't a disaster.
Yes, Alex has enjoyed his Cyborg smartphone...
...but recently, for a number of reasons - has been
considering the new myPhone.
Still not convinced, he needs perspective.
On the way to his new job, it's time for Alex to
connect with his trusted network of experts...
On the way to his new job, it's time for Alex to
connect with his trusted network of experts...
...his friends are quick to comment.
Things start to clear up for Alex when a peer
from his new firm adds a comment.
Alex's network of friends help him get past his initial doubts.
It's time for him to do his own research.
Everyone has their favorite place to begin. Alex jumps head first and swims through video reviews and feature lists of the newest product models.
After researching third-party sites, he's almost
there but not ready to commit just yet.
He decides to go directly to the product vendor to get the detail he needs.
It doesn't take long for the brand to reach out directly to Alex, in hopes of helping him with the next natural step.
Pleasantly surprised with a friendly chat, he considers it. Especially since a website that recognized his location just made this experience meaningful. Excited, he decides to continue his interaction with this brand.
His morning routine at the new firm passes quickly and Alex leaves for his appointment at the myPhone flagship store to see it for himself.
Impressed with the flagship store, his first physical interaction
with the brand leaves him in awe and excited for more.
Rose meets Alex in the store and hands him the phone.
He's blown away - it's love at first touch.
The interaction with the brand continues to impress him.
While purchasing the myPhone he was automatically eligible
for a 15% discount through the firm he works for.
Alex is happy. So he tweets about his new purchase.
Immediately his network responds.
Now, Alex wants to call Lisa. His phone contacts are not set up,
but it's simple to figure out. He calls support by clicking on
myPhone Help, the first number stored on his new phone.
The rep knows who he is and confirms his contacts are in the cloud.
Alex is relieved. This was seamless.
The great service continues. Alex is open to a deal suggested by the agent: 30% off an earpiece and a case for the new phone. He selects the case but needs more time for the earpiece.
Two weeks later, Alex finds Rose's business card and myPhone packaging. It has a description for self service via Twitter, inviting any questions regarding the product. He tries it.
Alex is taken aback at the immediate response from
the brand in his Twitter mentions.
He clicks on the link and a list of top five earpiece accessories
appear, with other relevant topics below. The immediacy and
relevance of the interaction is impressive.
He uses his phone app to purchase the earpiece and the
receipt comes to him through SMS.
Alex looks back at his interactions with the brand and realizes it didn't matter if he was buying the phone, or using the phone, each interaction was a great customer experience.
